02

Where general policies quietly leave gaps.

A standard commercial policy covers the clubhouse and the basics — and stops there. For a working golf club, the difference between a general policy and a specialist one shows up in the things that don't appear in a commercial template: greenkeeper plant, irrigation infrastructure, play cover, visiting societies, championship-scale events, capital projects in progress. Here is what that difference looks like, side by side.

What standard cover often misses

  • Clubhouse reinstatement at current build cost, not historic insured value
  • Greenkeeper plant and irrigation infrastructure, correctly scheduled
  • Play cover for members and competition across every course on site
  • Visiting society and event liability at the scale your club actually hosts
  • Event cancellation for championships, opens, and major fixtures
  • Newly acquired land, course developments, and capital projects mid-build

What a bespoke golf scheme covers

  • Full public liability including events, visiting societies and championship-scale gatherings
  • All course infrastructure correctly scheduled — not just the clubhouse
  • Play cover for members and competition across every course
  • Greenkeeper personal accident and plant cover, valued at current replacement cost
  • Event cancellation built for clubs hosting national and international fixtures
  • Comprehensive policy wording, built for golf — not adapted from commercial templates
